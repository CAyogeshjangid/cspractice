## Decisions

1. **Catch-up semantics**: a missed send-day (e.g. worker down at the 30-day
   mark) still dispatches as long as the due date is in the future; overdue
   rows never spawn reminders.
2. **No recipients → dispatch created DEAD immediately** with a clear error —
   surfaced in the dead-letter view instead of silently skipped (§4.6).
3. Rows marked filed / not-applicable are skipped at scheduling AND rechecked
   at delivery time (a row filed between the two never sends a stale nag).
4. Effective due date for reminders = override > extension > computed — read
   from stored values only; no date math outside the rules engine (C12).
5. Reminder config edits follow calendar-row RBAC (Executive assigned-only);
   dead-letter/retry are Manager+; email settings are Partner-only.
6. Encryption key for provider secrets is derived (SHA-256 → Fernet) from
   `JWT_SECRET` — no new required env var; documented in `security/crypto.py`.
